\section{Transceiver}



\subsection{Selection}
Ain't noboady designing a rf frontend from scratch,
an integrated seolution is the way to go.
Modules would be certified and less work,
but don't offer best flexibility and are boring af (for real).

Chips would require design and measurement of the antenna matching circuit,
but also could possibly compensate an antenna missmatch
and are only power limited by how much of an power amplifier you add.
\begin{itemize}
	\item CC1121/5 digital and well documented. Goat
	\item STM32 chips: SPIRIT1QTR, S2-LP, STM32WLE4/5xx well documented, STM in HF is iffy though
	\item ADF7020-1BCPZ-RL7 cheap Analog Devices. surley good (AD always cooking), but not very popular
	\item SA818-U looks like trash because it is. Analog FM. Very popular
	\item SX1261/2 150 MHz - 960 MHz Who tf is Semtech? Good on paper though
	\item Si446x (119–1050 MHz), looks perfect, reddit says: "\href{https://www.reddit.com/r/amateurradio/comments/qtlhz3/rf_tranceiver_ics_any_that_cover_2m_125m_70cm_and/}{most incredibly dense collection of bugs of any part I've ever worked with}".
	\item AX5043 Good things on the internet. N.A and Dead but apperently good
\end{itemize}

\subsection{CC1121/5}
Pin compatible Chips, CC1125 has a more narrow band than CC1121 so is to be preferred.
Can be paired with a power amplifier like Gali 74+ to get +38 dBm :) \href{https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9ZoO_AABzqU&t=313s}{youtube link}

So dipole is huge,
but monopole is also problematic.
It needs a ground plane, which can be solid or wires or a mesh.
If the groundplane radius is higher than $1/2 \lambda$ it's aproximately infinite 
and performance is optimal.
Groundplain changes gain and impedance.
