\section{Transceiver}



\subsection{Selection}
There are not many transcievers chips avaliable.
Chips would require design and measurement of the antenna matching circuit,
but also could possibly compensate an antenna missmatch.
\begin{itemize}
	\item CC1121/5 digital and well documented. Requires PA like SMA70-2
	\item ADF7020-1BCPZ-RL7 cheap Analog Devices.
	\item SA818-U looks like trash because it is. Analog FM
	\item SX1261/2 150 MHz - 960 MHz
	\item Si446x (119–1050 MHz), looks perfect, reddit says is a bit buggy.
	\item AX5043 Good things on the internet. N.A and Dead but apperently good
\end{itemize}

\section{Antenna}

\subsection{Mechanical considerations}
\paragraph{Requirements:}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Non ferro magnetic
	\item good impedance match
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Length estimate:}.

Often used for monopoles are $\lambda / 4$:
\[
	l_{\text{monopole}} = \frac{\lambda}{4} = \frac{c}{4f} = \frac{300}{4f\,[\text{MHz}]} = \frac{75}{f\,[\text{MHz}]}\,\text{m} = 0.5\;m\text{ at 150 MHz}
\]
Also commonly commercial used antennas are $5/8 \lambda$ lengths.
That is around 125 cm \href{https://www.pmr-funkgeraete.de/Funkgeraete/Amateurfunk/Zubehoer-AFU/Mobilantennen-AFU-KW-VHF-UHF/Diamond-MC-101-PL-Antennenstrahler-134-174-MHz::11365.html}{comertial example}.
